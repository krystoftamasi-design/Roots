Project ROOTS

Culture is a way of life that shapes us, defines us, and demonstrates what the renowned "golden Czech hands" can achieve. But what do Czech traditions look like in the 21st century? The ROOTS project, created by students of the Faculty of Multimedia Communications at Tomas Bata University in Zlín, answers this very question. Its core idea is to transform Czech and Slavic customs into a modern concept and to remind us of where we come from, where our roots lie, and what defines us as a society. This results in a fusion of history and youthful interpretation, opening a discussion about the preservation of cultural heritage in contemporary society.

The strength of the entire project lies in the unique interdisciplinary collaboration of five university studios. The entire process was initiated by students of Industrial Design, who designed a collection of porcelain products closely associated with Czech holidays. This primary line was subsequently expanded with original creations by students from the Footwear Design and Jewelry Design studios. To perfect the visual impact, Product Design students crafted custom-made packaging for each individual item. All these diverse works will ultimately be brought together by the Spatial Design studio, which will manage the complete architectural layout and presentation of the pieces at a joint exhibition.

The young creators drew inspiration from a wide range of sources, spanning from spiritual traditions to icons of Czech gastronomy. The collection reflects traditional holidays and customs, such as the Opening of the Springs, All Souls' Day, Carnival, and Candlemas. In addition to folklore, the designers pay tribute to typical Czech inventions and phenomena that are essential to our dining tables. The project offers a fresh perspective on the globally unique sugar cube, the phenomenon of the Czech open-faced sandwich, as well as traditional pastries and beer. Project ROOTS clearly proves that traditions are not a dead past, but a living inspiration for the present day.
